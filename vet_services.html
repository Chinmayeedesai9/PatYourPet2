1. Wellness Exams

Regular check-ups with a veterinarian to monitor your pet's overall health, address concerns, and administer vaccinations.

2. Vaccinations

Vaccinations protect pets from various diseases. Your vet will create a vaccination schedule based on your pet's age and risk factors.

3. Emergency Care

Veterinarians provide urgent care for injuries, poisoning, and sudden illnesses. Emergency clinics are available 24/7 for critical situations.

4. Surgery

Vets perform various surgical procedures, including spaying/neutering, tumor removal, and orthopedic surgeries.

5. Dental Care

Dental exams, cleanings, and treatments to maintain your pet's oral health.

6. Diagnostic Testing

X-rays, blood tests, and other diagnostics help diagnose illnesses and assess your pet's condition.

7. Prescription Medications

Vets prescribe medications to treat illnesses, infections, and manage chronic conditions.

8. Nutritional Counseling

Vets provide dietary recommendations tailored to your pet's specific needs, including weight management and special diets.

9. Behavioral Consultations

Address behavioral issues through consultations and, if necessary, behavioral modification plans.

10. Senior Pet Care

Specialized care for older pets, including screenings for age-related conditions and pain management.

11. Preventive Care

Preventative measures like flea/tick control and heartworm prevention to keep pets healthy.

12. Microchipping

Implanting a microchip under the skin for permanent identification.

13. Euthanasia and End-of-Life Care

Compassionate end-of-life services, including euthanasia and guidance on pet quality of life.

14. Specialist Referrals

For complex cases, vets may refer you to board-certified specialists in areas like cardiology, oncology, or neurology.

15. Rehabilitation and Physical Therapy

Therapy options for pets recovering from injuries or surgery.

16. Pet Grooming

Some veterinary clinics offer grooming services, including baths, haircuts, and nail trims.

17. Pet Boarding

Facilities that offer boarding services for pets when you're away, often with veterinary care available on-site.

18. Telemedicine

Some vets offer remote consultations for minor issues or follow-up care.
